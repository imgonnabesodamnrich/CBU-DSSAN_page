A Fault Diagnosis Framework for Gas Turbines under Highly Imbalanced Data

Paper Title: Highly imbalanced fault diagnosis of gas turbines via clustering-based downsampling and deep siamese self-attention network

Journal: Advanced Engineering Informatics, Vol. 54, 2022

DOI: 10.1016/j.aei.2022.101725

1. Research Background

Gas turbines are critical in aviation and industry. Due to high reliability, operational data is "highly imbalanced": massive normal samples but very few fault samples. Traditional models bias toward the majority, leading to high false-negative rates.

Furthermore, minor faults often overlap with normal data in the feature space. This paper proposes CBU-DSSAN, combining data-level clustering downsampling and model-level siamese attention networks.

2.1 Data Layer: Clustering-based Downsampling (CBU)

To avoid information loss in random downsampling, the paper uses a K-means strategy:

  1. Cluster the massive normal samples into multiple clusters.
  2. Extract cluster centers to represent normal states.
  3. Merge centers with rare fault samples to create a balanced set that retains distribution traits.

2.2 Model Layer: Deep Siamese Self-attention Network (DSSAN)

DSSAN combines two core mechanisms:

  • Siamese Network: Learns from paired samples using Contrastive Loss to separate classes in feature space.
  • Self-Attention Mechanism: Uses Multi-head Self-Attention to capture temporal dependencies in multidimensional time series.

3. Experimental Design and Data Processing

Real-world airline data was used, focusing on baseline deviations of EGT, speed, and fuel flow. Samples were built via sliding windows with five-fold cross-validation.

4. Conclusion

  1. Accuracy: Balanced Accuracy reached 92.26%, outperforming standalone baselines and other resampling strategies.
  2. Feature Separation: t-SNE shows distinct boundaries between classes in the DSSAN space.
  3. Efficiency: Inference time per sample is ~1.33ms, meeting real-time requirements.
